A Facebook Page can be the visible identity behind ads run from several accounts and by several partners. When the Page is restricted from advertising, replacing the ad account does not repair the affected object. The same Page relationship—and the same unresolved evidence—remains.

## Confirm whether the Page is actually restricted

Compare Page status, Business Support Home, Ads Manager delivery, and the views of authorized administrators. Separate:

- A rejected ad using the Page.
- An ad-account restriction.
- A person who lost advertising access.
- A Page publishing or Community Standards limitation.
- A Page-specific advertising restriction.
- A wider Business Portfolio restriction.

Record the Page ID, notice, policy reference, date, available review path, Page owner or controlling portfolio, and affected ad accounts.

## Audit the Page as a business identity

Review more than paid creative:

- Page name, category, About information, website, address, and contact channels.
- Page transparency and business ownership information.
- Organic posts, deleted or edited content, and current Page status.
- People and businesses with Facebook access or task access.
- Connected Instagram account, WhatsApp number, apps, shops, catalogs, and domains.
- Advertisers and agencies permitted to run ads with the Page.
- Brand authorization, franchise, reseller, or client relationships.

The Page should make sense to a reasonable user who moves from ad to profile to website. An unexplained mismatch between brand, operator, domain, and offer can be a management problem even when each asset exists legitimately.

## Look for repeated and distributed behavior

One Page may front ads from multiple accounts. Build a table of every account and partner that has used it, the campaigns run, markets targeted, policy incidents, and access dates.

If an agency reused a client Page across unrelated accounts, if a former partner retains access, or if several teams repeatedly submit variants of rejected material, the Page-level pattern can be broader than the last ad under review.

Do not delete Page history or remove relationships solely to make the review record look simpler. Remove unauthorized access and correct inaccurate information, but preserve an internal record of what changed and why.

## Remediate the real surface

Possible corrections include securing compromised administrators, removing unauthorized partners, correcting Page identity, resolving organic content violations, stopping ineligible offers, aligning the connected website, and implementing Page publishing controls.

Assign explicit owners for organic content, paid ads, community management, Page access, and business identity. A Page should not become shared infrastructure that nobody governs because every campaign belongs to a different team.

## Request review with Page-specific evidence

Identify the Page and decision, explain the confirmed cause or why the decision appears incorrect, list completed corrections, and attach concise evidence of ownership, authorization, security, or content fixes. Do not submit an ad-level explanation if the restriction concerns the Page’s wider activity.

For asset relationship analysis, continue with [Meta business asset management](/meta-ads/meta-business-asset-governance/). If the incident spans multiple Page and ad-account assets, use [Meta Business Manager restricted](/meta-ads/business-manager-restricted/). If only one creative was rejected, use [Meta ads rejected](/meta-ads/meta-ads-rejected/).

## Separate Page access types and duties

Document who has full Facebook access, who has task access, and which partner businesses can use the Page. Then map permissions to real responsibilities: publishing, community moderation, messaging, ads, insights, and settings.

Full control should not be the default for every agency employee or content contractor. Maintain at least two trusted brand-controlled administrators for ability to keep operating and review all external access on a schedule.

## Align organic and paid management

Users experience one Page, even if separate teams own posts and ads. Create shared rules for brand identity, product eligibility, claims, contests, user-generated content, links, and incident escalation. A paid-media approval cannot ignore public content that changes the apparent offer or business.

When organic content is removed or Page status changes, notify advertising teams. When paid ads trigger repeated concerns, notify Page and community owners. Both teams should use the same brand, license, and authorization records.

## Review Page history and origin and ownership history

Determine who created the Page, which brands and names it has represented, which portfolio controls it, and how partners have changed. A purchased, repurposed, or transferred Page can contain old administrators, content, followers, and identity signals unrelated to the current business.

Do not erase history to manufacture a clean appearance. If a legitimate acquisition or rebrand occurred, preserve records of the transaction and transition, update public information accurately, and remove obsolete access through a controlled process.

## Investigate impersonation and brand disputes

If the Page’s name, logo, domain, or product creates an ownership conflict, involve the real brand and legal owners. Preserve trademark, license, franchise, reseller, or client authorization. An agency’s ability to access a Page does not prove its right to represent the brand.

Avoid changing names or domains repeatedly during an active restriction. First establish the correct identity and the authorized process for correction or review.

## Validate recovery across advertisers

After a Page returns to advertising use, identify every ad account and partner that can publish through it. Review active and scheduled ads, destinations, catalogs, and message flows. Re-enable only authorized relationships.

Monitor Page status, new access, public-information changes, and rejected ads. A Page recovery can fail again if the same distributed publishing model remains uncontrolled.
