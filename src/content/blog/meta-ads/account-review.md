Most weak Meta review requests are written too early. The account stops delivering, a deadline feels urgent, and someone submits a short promise to follow every policy before the team has identified what Meta restricted.

Sincerity cannot repair an incorrect incident model. A useful review request is the final output of diagnosis, not the first troubleshooting action.

## Define the decision under review

Write a one-sentence case statement:

> On [date], Meta applied [exact decision] to [asset type and ID], citing [exact label], with [review option] available.

If the team cannot complete that sentence, it is not ready to submit. “Our ads were banned” may combine an ad rejection, payment failure, person-level access restriction, and business limitation into one inaccurate story.

Preserve the original notice, email, screenshots, asset IDs, timestamps, policy label, case ID, and available interface actions. Confirm whether Meta acted on an ad, person, Page, ad account, Business Portfolio, catalog, commerce account, or payment function.

Use [Meta Advertising Standards enforcement](/meta-ads/meta-advertising-standards-enforcement/) if the scope remains unclear.

## Build one incident timeline

The timeline should cover the period before and after the decision:

| Time | Event | Actor | Asset | Evidence | Relevance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Before | Ad, web, access, billing, or business change | Named person/system | ID | Change record | Potential signal |
| Decision | Restriction or rejection | Meta | ID | Notice | Confirmed event |
| After | Containment or correction | Named owner | ID | Screenshot/log | Remediation |
| Review | Case submitted or updated | Named owner | Case ID | Submission copy | Communication |

Use platform records, internal approvals, web deployments, security logs, invoices, and contracts. Do not fill gaps with confident guesses.

## Separate three evidence states

- **Observed:** the record directly shows it.
- **Confirmed:** investigation and evidence establish it.
- **Inferred:** it is a plausible explanation but remains unproven.

This prevents a theory from turning into an unnecessary admission. It also enables a truthful request when the team believes the decision is incorrect: explain the relevant facts and evidence without inventing a violation to appear cooperative.

## Perform root-cause analysis at the correct scope

Review the system surrounding the affected object:

- Creative, copy, targeting, destination, forms, messages, and catalogs.
- Page and business identity, domain, product, price, claims, and disclosures.
- People, partners, permissions, sessions, authentication, and recent invitations.
- Payment methods, payer authority, failed charges, balances, and unfamiliar transactions.
- Connected or previously used assets and repeated policy history.

Ask why the issue passed internal controls, not only what the issue was. If a misleading price appeared because a feed rule overwrote the website, editing one ad is not a complete correction. If unauthorized campaigns appeared through a compromised administrator, removing the ads without securing the control path is not recovery.

## Create an evidence-to-claim matrix

Every material sentence in the review should be supportable.

| Claim | Evidence | What it establishes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Legal business identity | Current registry record | Entity existence and details |
| Right to use brand/Page | Ownership or authorization | Relationship to public identity |
| Operator is legitimate | Employment/agency authorization | Reason for access |
| Security incident contained | Session/access record and completed controls | Unauthorized path removed |
| Destination corrected | Deployment record and screenshots | User experience changed |
| Billing reconciled | Meta activity, invoice, and bank reference | Payer and transaction explanation |

Do not overload the case with unrelated files. Relevance and internal consistency matter more than document volume.

## Write the submission as a correction record

A practical structure is:

1. Asset and exact decision.
2. Request: review an apparent error or reconsider after remediation.
3. Confirmed cause or relevant facts.
4. Corrective actions, with dates and affected assets.
5. Preventive controls and named ownership.
6. Short index of supporting evidence.

Avoid guarantees, emotional pressure, accusations, false documents, or copied templates that do not match the case. Use one factual voice across Business Support Home, email, chat, and internal records.

Meta’s [ad review and support guide](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/review-policy-guidelines) directs eligible advertisers to Business Support Home for review of incorrectly restricted assets. Follow the review mechanism shown for the actual decision; do not assume every case has the same route or timetable.

## Govern follow-up and post-review operation

Store the submitted text, attachments, time, case ID, response, and any later evidence. New information should clarify the same record, not restart the story. If access is restored, stage campaign reactivation, monitor security and billing, and verify that the corrected templates and destinations remain live.

If the request is unsuccessful, compare the response with the case record and determine whether any authorized review or support option remains. Do not create replacement assets to bypass the decision.

For the wider recovery program, continue with [Meta Ads appeal and recovery](/meta-ads/appeal-and-recovery/).

## Handle uncertainty without weakening the case

Not every platform decision exposes its full reasoning. The team may have a clear notice but no confirmed internal defect. In that situation, describe the audit scope, compliant facts, and supporting evidence; do not manufacture a root cause.

A useful uncertainty statement is specific: which ads, destinations, people, payments, and relationships were reviewed; what the records showed; and which evidence supports the request for reconsideration. “We checked everything” is not auditable.

If several plausible concerns remain, resolve every confirmed weakness before submission and distinguish the unresolved hypotheses. This produces a more trustworthy record than choosing the theory that seems easiest to appeal.

## Create a case evidence index

Use stable filenames and a short index:

| Item | Description | Relevant claim | Date/source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A01 | Restriction notice | Decision and asset | Business Support Home |
| A02 | Access export | Authorized people and partners | Business settings |
| A03 | Landing-page capture | Corrected offer and disclosure | Production site |
| A04 | Registry or authorization | Business/brand relationship | Issuing party |
| A05 | Billing reconciliation | Payer and transaction facts | Meta and finance records |

Redact irrelevant personal or financial data. Never alter evidence to make fields agree. When records legitimately differ, explain why.

## Coordinate agencies, clients, and vendors

Decide who is authorized to submit and who supplies each fact. The client should confirm business identity, product, licenses, and customer experience. The agency should confirm campaign, access, and operational actions. Vendors should confirm their own system changes.

One case owner should reconcile these contributions before anything reaches support. A provider should not speculate about a client’s legal facts, and a client should not deny campaign actions recorded under an authorized agency workflow.

## Measure review process quality

Track time to scope classification, time to containment, completeness of the first evidence packet, contradictory contacts, repeated submissions, decision result, and recurrence. Do not judge the team solely by reinstatement rate; correct denials and incorrect approvals both exist. Judge whether the organization presented an accurate, timely, controlled record and learned from the incident.
