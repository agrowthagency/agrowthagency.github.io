The most sophisticated campaign cannot make an ineligible offer eligible.

Yet restricted businesses often begin Google Ads planning with keywords, creatives, and budget. Certification is left to the launch checklist, and policy review is treated as an obstacle that appears after media strategy is complete.

The sequence should be reversed. In regulated or abuse-prone categories, the first campaign decision is whether a specific entity may promote a specific product, through a specific domain, to a specific location and audience, under the current Google Ads requirements.

Until that combination is established, campaign design is speculative.

## “Restricted business” is not one policy state

Google Ads distinguishes prohibited content, restricted content, restricted formats and features, and other restricted businesses. Within those areas, an offer can be:

- Prohibited entirely.
- Allowed only in listed locations.
- Allowed only for licensed or authorized entities.
- Subject to Google certification or an external verification partner.
- Limited by age, audience, format, keyword, or personalization rules.
- Eligible with limitations rather than fully disapproved.
- Allowed for one product subtype but not another that appears commercially similar.

Do not infer eligibility from a competitor’s visible ad. The competitor may have a different license, certification, entity type, account, target location, product classification, or legacy approval. An ad impression is evidence that an ad served, not that another advertiser qualifies under the same conditions.

## Build an eligibility matrix before opening campaigns

Create one row for every material offer and target market:

| Control | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Product | What exactly is being promoted, including fulfillment and monetization? |
| Advertiser | Which legal entity is responsible for the ad and customer relationship? |
| Authority | Which regulator, license, registration, exemption, or authorization applies? |
| Geography | Where will ads target and where can the product legally be supplied? |
| Certification | Does Google or an external partner require an application? |
| Account | Which customer ID and manager relationship hold the approval? |
| Domain | Who owns and operates the destination submitted for certification? |
| Audience | What age, personalization, or remarketing restrictions apply? |
| Creative | Which claims, terms, images, and disclosures are restricted? |
| Change control | Which changes require review or recertification? |

The matrix forces the business to classify the real offer rather than the label sales uses. “Finance,” “health,” “gaming,” and “support” are not precise enough for policy eligibility.

## License, verification, and certification answer different questions

A government license establishes authority under a legal regime. Advertiser verification establishes identity or business information. Google certification establishes eligibility to promote a covered product under Google’s process. An external compliance partner may validate additional requirements.

One does not automatically replace the others.

For example, financial services verification can be location-specific and may require regulator information. Healthcare promotion can require external certification plus an application to Google for certain products. Gambling eligibility depends on product type, country, local authorization, account health, domain requirements, and certification.

Google’s current [certification application guidance](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/16114090?hl=en) organizes applications by product and service. Use the live policy and application route for the exact category; do not reuse an old form or assume an approval applies to another account or country.

## Geography is part of the product

Restricted-business policy cannot be separated from targeting. The same service may be eligible in one country, require different evidence in another, and be prohibited elsewhere.

Map three geographies:

1. The advertiser’s legal and licensed location.
2. The user locations targeted by the campaign.
3. The locations where the service is delivered or available.

Then account for location settings, cross-border fulfillment, VPN or travel scenarios, localized pages, and campaign expansion. Broadening targets is a compliance change, not merely a media optimization.

Google’s June 2026 financial-services update, for example, introduced verification requirements for additional EEA markets with enforcement beginning on specified dates. The practical lesson is larger than one update: country eligibility is versioned and must be monitored.

## Domain control is part of certification integrity

Restricted-business applications commonly bind eligibility to a website or app. The destination must represent the certified entity and covered activity.

Google’s 2026 gambling updates emphasize good policy health and domain ownership or operation requirements, including restrictions involving free hosting and unrelated or third-party-root subdomains. That illustrates a general control principle: certification should not be treated as a portable permission that can be attached to any funnel.

Before applying, confirm:

- The advertiser owns or is authorized to operate the domain.
- The legal business and licenses are visible where required.
- The submitted offer matches the live destination.
- Country routing does not expose unapproved products.
- Affiliates and aggregators are classified correctly.
- Tracking and redirects preserve the approved domain and content.
- Material changes trigger an eligibility review.

Certification for one account, domain, product, or geography should not be assumed to cover another.

## Agencies need an authority chain, not borrowed approval

An agency may prepare campaigns and support applications, but the regulated advertiser remains central. The account should be able to show who is licensed, who supplies the service, who owns the destination, who pays, and what authority the agency has.

In some programs, manager-level or agency-related certification routes may exist subject to eligibility. In others, the first party or an authorized advertiser must apply or authorize a third party. The current application rules control.

Maintain:

- Client authorization.
- License and regulator record.
- Certification identifier and scope.
- Customer IDs covered.
- Domains or apps covered.
- Countries and product types covered.
- Renewal or expiry dates.
- Material-change and revocation procedures.

Never substitute the agency’s documents simply because the client’s approval is difficult. Borrowed identity can turn an eligibility problem into verification, misrepresentation, or Circumventing systems risk.

## Example: certified category, uncertified operation

Consider a composite financial platform licensed in one market. Its agency copies the successful campaign into several neighboring countries, localizes the landing page, and uses the same Google Ads account. The team assumes the original license and advertiser verification are sufficient.

One target market requires a separate verification process. Another does not permit the promoted product subtype. The localized page also presents a partner as the service provider without explaining the relationship.

The failure is not a missing disclaimer. The campaign combines an unverified geography, an ineligible product classification, and an unclear provider.

The correct response pauses the affected markets, maps eligibility country by country, separates allowed and disallowed offers, obtains the required approvals through the correct entity, aligns destination disclosures, and introduces approval before geographic expansion.

## Certification must be operated after approval

Approval is the beginning of a controlled state. Monitor:

- License validity and regulator status.
- Changes to legal entity, ownership, or business model.
- New products and claims.
- Domain and app changes.
- Target-country expansion.
- Manager and customer-ID changes.
- Policy health and certification notices.
- Country-specific policy updates.

Assign one owner to maintain the eligibility matrix and evidence archive. Campaign teams should not have to interpret a complex regulatory chain during an urgent launch.

The deeper principle is simple: restricted-business advertising is an authorization system. Performance teams can optimize only inside the boundary created by product eligibility, legal authority, platform certification, and truthful representation.

When that boundary is explicit, compliance becomes part of market design. When it is assumed, every campaign change can unknowingly redefine the business Google is being asked to approve.

Restricted-business eligibility depends on a consistent advertiser. Use the [Google Ads advertiser verification guide](/google-ads/google-ads-advertiser-verification/) for identity and relationship controls, and the [Google agency account guide](/google-ads/google-agency-ad-account/) when an agency or manager participates in applications, billing, or account ownership. Where Google asks how the parties actually deliver the service, keep those facts aligned with the [business-operations verification record](/google-ads/google-ads-business-operations-verification/).
