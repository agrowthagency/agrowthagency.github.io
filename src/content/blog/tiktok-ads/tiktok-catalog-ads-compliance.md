Correcting a product inside Catalog Manager can create the illusion of resolution. If the next scheduled feed writes the defect back, nothing was fixed.

Catalog compliance begins at the authoritative data source.

## Map catalog architecture

Record catalog and Business Center owner, advertiser accounts, product sources, schedules, supplemental feeds, transformation rules, country/language sets, pixels or apps, shops, and destination templates. Identify which system owns ID, title, category, image, description, price, currency, availability, brand, and URL.

TikTok’s [catalog diagnostics guidance](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/list-of-catalog-product-issues-and-suggested-actions) includes duplicate IDs, inaccessible images or product links, malformed feeds, and price or inventory inconsistency between product data and landing pages. Technical correctness and policy eligibility both matter.

## Separate incident classes

| Class | Example | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Schema/format | Missing field, duplicate ID, invalid file | Feed engineering |
| Access | TikTok cannot fetch feed, image, or product URL | Web/infrastructure |
| Data truth | Price or inventory differs from page | Commerce operations |
| Product eligibility | Prohibited or restricted product | Business/compliance |
| Review state | Product disapproved or awaiting review | Catalog/ads owner |
| Catalog lifecycle | Cold/frozen or stale feed | Catalog operations |

Do not appeal a malformed feed as though it were a policy decision.

## Sample by system, not convenience

Inspect rejected products and representative items by supplier, category, template, market, price rule, inventory state, language, and source feed. Trace each displayed value back to its source and transformation.

If one rule caused the issue, query every item touched by it. One approved sample does not validate the batch.

## Reconcile the customer experience

Compare product, image, variant, price, currency, discount, availability, seller, shipping, returns, and checkout total. Monitor stale availability, low-price variants presented as universal, and localized pages that do not match feed currency.

Supplier contracts should require authentic, eligible products, accurate data, fulfillment, and prompt reporting of license, recall, brand, or inventory changes. The advertiser cannot outsource the truth while continuing to distribute it.

## Treat feed changes as releases

Require test sets, peer review for high-impact rules, post-sync monitoring, rollback, and a named owner. Store before-and-after product records. If a manual correction is necessary, update the source and verify the next sync.

Before restarting a dormant or frozen catalog, request fresh data and validate it. TikTok’s catalog documentation notes that inactive catalogs can have reduced review activity and that scheduled feed state matters when reactivating.

## Prepare appeals with product evidence

For a product believed to be disapproved incorrectly, preserve the diagnostic, product ID, source data, landing page, category eligibility, brand authorization, and required qualifications. Appeal the product decision through the available path; do not submit generic company documents unrelated to it.

Use [TikTok Ads industry eligibility](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-ads-industry-eligibility/) for category entry and [TikTok Ads landing page requirements](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-ads-landing-page-requirements/) for feed-to-page consistency.

## Govern suppliers and marketplaces

Verify supplier identity, product authorization, authenticity, industry eligibility, inventory, fulfillment, returns, and complaint process. Contracts should require accurate data and prompt notification of recall, brand dispute, certificate expiry, or product change.

Build a rapid product-disable path independent of the normal merchandising calendar. The advertiser remains responsible for what its feed distributes.

## Reconcile price and variant logic

Document how base price, sale price, reference price, currency, tax, shipping, subscription, variant, and inventory fields are produced. Test low-priced variants, out-of-stock defaults, regional catalogs, and checkout totals.

Do not promote the cheapest unavailable variant as the product’s normal price. Material conditions should be visible before commitment.

## Monitor diagnostics as operations

Assign owners and response times for feed fetch failure, image failure, invalid links, stale schedules, price or inventory mismatch, disapproved products, and frozen status. Trend errors by source and rule.

A dashboard nobody reviews is not a catalog control. Connect recurring defects to engineering or supplier remediation.

## Use a composite feed incident

A regional rule converts prices but not sale dates, while inventory sync lags. Ads display an expired discount and lead to an unavailable product. Manually correcting three rejected items cannot fix the next thousand.

Pause the affected rule, preserve diagnostic and feed versions, correct transformation and timing, query all touched SKUs, validate destinations, and then resync.

## Control catalog access

Limit who can add product sources, change schedules and rules, disable products, connect pixels/apps, or assign catalogs to advertisers. Record partner access and remove suppliers or agencies after termination.

## Validate restart

Before delivery resumes, confirm source freshness, diagnostics, representative product review, price/inventory match, landing pages, eligibility, advertiser assignment, pixel/app connection, and product sets. Monitor the next scheduled sync so a source regression does not undo the repair.

Catalog compliance is a data-quality discipline with policy consequences. The source system, not the last interface edit, determines whether the correction survives.
