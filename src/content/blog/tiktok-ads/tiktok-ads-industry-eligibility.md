Restricted industries usually begin TikTok planning in the wrong place. Teams develop creator concepts, hooks, and budgets before establishing whether the actual advertiser and offer can enter the target market.

Eligibility should be the first media decision.

## Build an entry matrix

TikTok’s [Industry Entry overview](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/tiktok-advertising-policies-industry-entry) routes advertisers to policies for financial services, gambling, healthcare, alcohol, politics, weight management, intellectual property, dangerous products, and other categories, plus market-specific and certificate requirements.

For each launch, document:

1. Exact product or service and customer outcome.
2. Legal advertiser and brand relationship.
3. Country or subnational market.
4. Age and audience restrictions.
5. Required license, certificate, or TikTok qualification.
6. Creative and claims.
7. Landing page and after-conversion experience.
8. Ad account, TikTok identity, and catalog in scope.

An eligible company does not make every product eligible. A license in one country does not authorize neighboring markets. An approved ad from another account does not transfer its facts.

## Separate four states

- **Prohibited:** the planned activity cannot be advertised in that context.
- **Restricted:** it may advertise only under defined conditions.
- **Qualification pending:** required documents or platform approval are incomplete.
- **Operationally ineligible:** the chosen account, identity, market, destination, or format does not satisfy the conditions.

This vocabulary prevents teams from calling every rejection a creative problem.

## Map complex business roles

Lead generators, marketplaces, franchises, affiliates, agencies, and platforms can separate the advertiser, brand owner, licensed provider, payer, website operator, data recipient, and service fulfiller. Document each role and the contracts or authorization connecting them.

Do not submit an agency’s certificate as if it licenses the client’s activity. Do not hide the real beneficiary behind a generic educational landing page.

## Control certificate lifecycle

Maintain holder, number, issuing authority, permitted activity, market, product, accounts/domains, issue date, expiry, renewal owner, and evidence. Revalidate after entity, domain, TikTok identity, agency, account, product, or market changes.

Block campaign duplication into unapproved geographies. Country selection in Ads Manager is not evidence that the offer is eligible.

## Monitor the real business

Product formulation, prices, licenses, provider relationships, complaints, inventory, fulfillment, and law can change after approval. Connect business changes to campaign review and stop triggers. Marketing cannot keep advertising facts current if operations never notify it.

If the activity is clearly prohibited, another appeal is not a strategy. Retire the campaign and prevent reuse. Use appeal for a decision believed to be incorrect or after genuine, permissible remediation.

For identity evidence, continue with [TikTok business verification](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-business-verification/). For claim design, use [TikTok misleading and false content](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-ads-misleading-content/).

## Classify composite offers

A software product may include financial services, a wellness brand may make medical claims, a marketplace may host regulated sellers, and a lead generator may connect users to licensed providers. Classify what the ad causes the user to do, not the company’s broad industry label.

Trace ad, destination, form, payment, data transfer, service delivery, and customer contract. Identify the real provider and beneficiary at each step.

## Build country launch gates

Require approval for product legality, TikTok policy, age, language, certificate, advertiser, identity, claims, landing page, payment, fulfillment, customer support, and data collection. Store the effective policy date and reviewer.

Configure campaign naming and build controls so automated expansion or duplication cannot add an unapproved country silently.

## Govern creators in restricted categories

Provide creators a market-specific fact sheet: allowed product, claim boundaries, required disclosures, prohibited demonstrations, age considerations, licensed entity, destination, and approval process. Review final video, audio, text, and account identity.

Authenticity does not mean improvising regulated claims. A creator’s personal experience cannot automatically substantiate typical product outcomes.

## Use a composite eligibility case

A licensed financial platform expands an approved campaign to a neighboring market using the same agency account and localized landing page. The business assumes its verification and home-market license travel with the campaign.

The error is not translation. The new combination has a different legal entity/market relationship, qualification, disclosures, targeting, and possibly product eligibility. Campaign duplication preceded entry analysis.

## Record why a launch is rejected internally

Use clear dispositions: prohibited by TikTok, not lawful in market, certificate missing, advertiser mismatch, evidence insufficient, destination incomplete, or operational control unavailable. This prevents future teams from reopening the same idea under a new creative name.

## Monitor policy change

Assign owners to review Industry Entry and market-specific requirements before launches and on a schedule. Date the internal matrix. When policy changes, find every affected account, campaign, creative, certificate, and destination.

For product-feed campaigns, carry the eligibility decision into the SKU-level controls used for [TikTok catalog ads](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-catalog-ads-compliance/) so an approved industry does not conceal an ineligible product.
