Verification failures are often blamed on the document. The deeper issue is usually the relationship between the document and the operation.

A genuine certificate can identify one legal company while the ad account promotes another brand, the website names a supplier, an agency submits the form, and a different entity pays. Every relationship may be legitimate. Verification still needs them to be intelligible.

## Distinguish verification scopes

TikTok’s current [business-verification guidance](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/about-business-verification/) describes verification through Ads Manager or Business Center, and separately discusses company and legal-representative information. It also describes shared verification for linked Business Accounts in eligible flows.

Record what is being verified:

- Advertiser business information in Ads Manager.
- Business Center company information.
- Legal representative identity.
- A linked Business Account using shared verification.

Do not market one verified object as proof that every related account, product, or client is approved.

## Create a canonical identity record

| Field | Required answer |
| --- | --- |
| Legal name | Exact registered entity and suffix |
| Registration | Number, jurisdiction, status, issue/expiry dates |
| Address | Supported business address and any legitimate variation |
| Website | Controlled domain describing the business and offer |
| Representative | Named person, role, authority, ID where required |
| Brand | Ownership, license, franchise, or agency relationship |
| Advertiser | Account and Business Center IDs in scope |
| Qualifications | Industry license/certificate, market, product, expiry |

Normalize abbreviations, translations, suite numbers, legacy names, and parent/subsidiary relationships without altering documents.

## Match evidence to propositions

A registry record proves legal facts. A representative document proves identity. A contract may establish authority. A license establishes only the activity, entity, geography, and period it covers. A website demonstrates the public operating story.

Build an evidence index showing what each item proves, its date, source, and any discrepancy. Store sensitive documents under controlled access rather than in campaign chats.

## Diagnose rejection systematically

Preserve the rejection state and compare submitted values with accepted current documents. Check legibility, completeness, validity, country-specific document requirements, certificate number, business name, website, representative information, and submitter permissions.

Do not rotate through unrelated documents or change business facts until something passes. Correct confirmed data-entry errors and explain legitimate differences. TikTok’s current guidance provides a Request Review path from Account setup for continuing verification problems; use the path shown to the account.

## Treat change as reverification risk

Mergers, rebrands, entity changes, agency transitions, domain migrations, and new representatives can make the record stale. Maintain an owner and change triggers. The business—not a departing provider—should retain the evidence and administrative capability to explain its identity.

Use [TikTok Ads industry eligibility](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-ads-industry-eligibility/) for qualifications and [TikTok Business Center restricted](/tiktok-ads/business-center-restricted/) when identity problems sit inside wider portfolio enforcement.

## Distinguish advertiser roles

Map legal advertiser, brand owner, website operator, product or service provider, license holder, agency, payer, data recipient, and customer-contracting entity. For each difference, retain the contract, corporate record, license, or authorization that connects the roles.

The goal is not to force every role into one organization. It is to ensure that verification does not become a mask for a business TikTok and users cannot identify.

## Coordinate corporate transitions

Before a merger, rebrand, new subsidiary, or agency transition changes platform data, document old and new entities, effective date, assets, accounts, domains, representatives, qualifications, billing, and customer disclosures. Preserve evidence of continuity.

Changing many identity fields at once without a transition record makes a legitimate event harder to explain and harder to reverse operationally.

## Govern shared verification carefully

Where TikTok supports shared verification between a verified Business Center and linked Business Accounts, inventory which accounts receive that state, their owner, brand, purpose, and relationship to the verified company. Remove or reassess sharing when ownership or relationship changes.

Inheritance of a verification status does not make every account’s content, qualification, market, or commercial behavior compliant.

Complete it for name, number, address, website, representative, and certificate. This turns a vague rejection into a controlled comparison and prevents document roulette.

## Treat verification as governance output

After approval, store verified object, entity, representative, documents, submission date, reviewer state, linked accounts, and change triggers. Schedule document expiry review. Ensure more than one authorized business-controlled administrator understands the record.

Verification is strongest when it reflects a business already governed coherently; it is weakest when used to compensate for unclear ownership.
