Campaigns can be rebuilt. A disputed advertiser account, inaccessible TikTok identity, orphaned pixel, or provider-controlled Business Center can interrupt the operating system behind every campaign.

## Inventory control, ownership, and responsibility

Record Business Centers, advertiser accounts, TikTok accounts, organization accounts, pixels, catalogs, audiences, shops, apps, payment portfolios, members, and partners. For each asset capture platform ID, technical controller, contractual owner, business owner, operator, dependencies, backup administrator, and offboarding action.

TikTok’s [Business Center ad-account guidance](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/manage-ad-accounts-business-center?lang=en) distinguishes accounts owned by the Business Center from partner-owned accounts and notes that transfer availability can depend on customer eligibility. Access is not ownership, and commercial funding is neither.

## Use deliberate access structures

Prefer named membership, scoped roles, partner requests, and TikTok identity authorization over shared credentials. A client-owned advertiser account with agency access creates different continuity from an agency-created or provider-controlled account. Document the model before spend.

For TikTok accounts, separate “Deliver ads” from broader “Manage account” permissions. Determine whether content can appear organically, be used only in ads, or support messaging and LIVE features.

## Map blast radius

Represent relationships:

`member/partner → Business Center → advertiser/data/identity/finance asset → campaign/customer process`

Identify single administrators, shared balances, identities linked to many advertiser accounts, common pixels, catalogs used across markets, and provider-controlled assets. Centralization can improve oversight while increasing impact if the center fails.

## Make changes auditable

Require requests and approvals for admins, partners, finance roles, advertiser access, identity linkage, transfers, payment changes, data sharing, and destructive actions. Record start and review dates. Reconcile the live environment with HR, contracts, client lists, finance, and the asset register quarterly.

Measure assets without a confirmed owner, privileged users without strong authentication, dormant partners, overdue access reviews, single-admin dependencies, and unresolved ownership conflicts.

## Design offboarding at onboarding

Specify campaigns, reports, creatives, TikTok identities, pixels, audiences, catalogs, leads, balances, invoices, support tickets, and data retention. At exit, establish replacement client admins, reconcile money, preserve records, remove members and partners, unlink only the intended assets, and verify access from the owner’s account.

Do not promise transferability that TikTok does not make available to that customer. Where an asset cannot be moved, the contract should define the dependency and contingency honestly.

## Recover governance after enforcement

If Business Center is restricted, preserve the graph before mass removal. Secure unauthorized paths, but keep an internal change ledger. Explain legitimate complexity with contracts and ownership records rather than trying to make the portfolio appear artificially simple.

Use [TikTok Business Center restricted](/tiktok-ads/business-center-restricted/) for incidents, [TikTok Ads account security](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-ads-account-security/) for privileged paths, and [TikTok agency ads accounts](/tiktok-ads/tiktok-agency-ads-account/) for commercial structures.

## Define controls by asset class

Advertiser accounts need campaign, finance, and policy governance. TikTok identities need public-brand and content authorization. Pixels and audiences need data purpose and sharing controls. Catalogs need feed ownership and product accuracy. Payment portfolios need allocation and reconciliation.

For each class, define who may create, connect, share, edit, transfer, unlink, or delete; which approval is required; and what evidence must remain.

## Govern shared data assets

Document data owner/controller, collection purpose, permitted advertiser accounts and partners, client separation, retention, transfer, and termination for pixels, audiences, apps, catalogs, and leads. Platform permission is necessary but not sufficient for lawful data use.

Do not connect data across clients merely because the technology permits it or performance might improve.

## Protect high-impact changes

Partner removal, account transfer, identity unlinking, pixel disconnection, catalog deletion, and payment changes can disrupt many campaigns. Require impact assessment against the dependency graph, second approval, verified IDs, change window, and validation.

Preserve records before destructive actions. A screenshot is not a backup, but it can establish the pre-change state and support reconstruction.

## Maintain asset provenance

Record creator, intended advertiser, original Business Center, ownership changes, agencies, transfers, and current authority. For provider-sourced assets, identify every upstream controller and limitation.

An unclear history is not proof of abuse. It is proof the business cannot yet explain its own operating rights.

## Run quarterly reconciliation

Compare the live Business Center with HR, contracts, client inventory, finance, TikTok identities, domains, and data systems. Investigate assets present in only one record. Validate backup admins and sample whether permissions still match duties.

Close with an exception register containing owner and due date. Quietly editing the spreadsheet does not resolve a live control gap.

## Model resilience without artificial separation

Assess impact if a Business Center, admin, partner, payment portfolio, TikTok identity, or shared data asset becomes unavailable. Build backups and ownership clarity. Do not fragment assets or create unrelated structures merely to obscure genuine relationships.
